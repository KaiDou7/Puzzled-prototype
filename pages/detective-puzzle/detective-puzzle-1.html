1. Three Spies

Three spies, suspected as double agents, speak as follows when questioned: Albert: "Bertie is a mole." Bertie: "Cedric is a mole." Cedric: "Bertie is lying." Assuming that moles lie, other agents tell the truth, and there is just one mole among the three.

Who is the mole?

Bertie Albert Cedric No one
Next

Answer:Bertie

If we consider Cedric is not a mole, therefore Cedric is telling the truth, which inturn means that Bertie is lying. Which proves our hypothesis of Cedric being an agent as Bertie says Cedric is lying but that is a lie hence, he’s telling the truth. Our hypothesis then concludes that Albert is an agent as well, that means he must be telling the truth, and as he says Bertie is a mole it solidifies out deduction.

Note: It is usually said that the person who tries to blame something on others is usually the culprit in this case it could’ve been either Albert or Bertie. It just helps you narrow down your suspects at times.
